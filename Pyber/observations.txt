Trends:

1. The highest number of drivers are in urban cities; this is due to the high concentration of people in a small area and larger amounts of supply due to the large market of riders.

2. The average fare is higher in rural cities while supply and demand in rural areas are both low, this is due to scarcity of drivers and therefore consumers must pay a premium to access a *rare* service.

3. The highest number of riders are in urban cities; this is also due to the high concentration of people in a small area and higher demand for rideshare because of externalities like traffic in congested cities.
